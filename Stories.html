I begin by thanking God, for without His grace and guidance this journey would not have been possible. My time with Polaeco has been more than an experience, it has been a blessing filled with love, community, and passion. To me, Polaeco has never been just an organisation, it has been a family, a home of vision and unity, where hearts and hands joined together to build something greater than ourselves.

In every meeting, every project, and every shared dream, I have witnessed the beauty of togetherness. We supported one another in times of challenge, celebrated one another’s growth, and stood side by side with the belief that our efforts could bring light to others. The spirit of Polaeco is alive not only in its work, but in the compassion and dedication of its people.

What has touched me most deeply is the passion that flows through all that we do. It is a passion that turns obstacles into stepping stones, that transforms ordinary tasks into lasting memories, and that reminds us that love is the strongest foundation of change. It lives in the laughter during planning, the resilience during trials, and the assurance that none of us walked this journey alone.

As I reflect, I know that I carry the spirit of Polaeco with me into every path ahead. The lessons of community, the gift of shared purpose, and the depth of love I have found here will remain within me always. I am grateful beyond measure for every soul who has walked this path with me, for together we built more than projects, we built bonds that cannot be undone. Polaeco will forever live in my heart as a testimony of God’s grace, of love in action, and of passion turned into purpose.

Sincerely,
C.L.A Williams

I have chosen to focus Polaeco on young women as I see them as the most underestimated force in society. A force, that once awakened, can potentially redefine the moral and political compass of nations. Young women carry a unique consciousness derived from being in a possibility-constrained existence. We are raised to dream, but we sleep within limits. We learn how to be resilient while navigating with the tension of being visible yet unseen; audible yet ignored.

This consciousness, a synergism of experience, generational knowledge, and untraced ambition is not weakness. It is an organizational advantage. Young women have a unique understanding of injustice not as a bias but as lived experience; they experience inequality in their pay, their safety, their right to be heard in meetings they have stressed to attend. This locates them in a type of moral clarity and sense of empathy that results in leadership being a quest for justice not power.

When young women lead, they make contributions beyond filling positions; they change the parameters of leadership itself. They offer a perspective that challenges what is taken-for-granted, they connect to care rather than exploit community, and they understand that progress is only progress if it is shared.

Polaeco is my way of saying: this consciousness is not a problem; it is the template of a better world. I believe that when young women rise, societies rise with them; and I am dedicated to making sure that platforms, mentorship, and networks do not provide small doors of opportunities, but do make being upwardly mobile unstoppable.

Queen Wana

My name is Queen Wana, and I am a 22-year-old Honour student in Public Governance. Being a part of POLAECO in 2024 as a Projects Committee Member and Projects Coordinator was a transformative experience that greatly contributed to my personal and professional development. Through this dynamic organisation, I gained the confidence to step into leadership roles and acquired critical skills in strategic planning, event coordination, and the development of self.

POLAECO gave me the platform to independently plan and execute events, allowing me to apply theory to practice in real-world settings. They hosted impactful programmes—such as interview readiness sessions, CV-building workshops, and leadership webinars in collaboration with esteemed organisations like WENOSA—which played a pivotal role in nurturing my confidence and affirming the value of women’s empowerment.

More than just an academic experience, POLAECO empowered me to lead with purpose in all areas of my life. As a student aspiring to make a difference in the public sector, the organisation’s public speaking bootcamps helped refine my communication skills and ignited a passion for civic engagement and policy work.

POLAECO is more than a leadership incubator—it is a movement that instils belief in the potential of young women to lead, influence, and excel. I am grateful for the opportunity to have been part of such a visionary space. It not only sharpened my leadership abilities but inspired me to be an agent of change within my community and beyond.

Yours Sincerely,
Queen Wana
